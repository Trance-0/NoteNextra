Subgroup of cyclic group is cyclic

Every subgroup of a cyclic group is cyclic.

Order of subgroup of cyclic group

If a∈Ga\in Ga∈G and ∣⟨a⟩∣|\langle a\rangle|∣⟨a⟩∣ be the smallest positive nnn such that an=ea^n=ean=e, then ⟨a⟩={e,a,a2,⋯ ,an−1}\langle a\rangle=\{e,a,a^2,\cdots,a^{n-1}\}⟨a⟩={e,a,a2,⋯,an−1} and am1=am2  ⟺  m1=m2mod  na^{m_1}=a^{m_2}\iff m_1=m_2\mod nam1​=am2​⟺m1​=m2​modn. (nnn divides m1−m2m_1-m_2m1​−m2​)

Suppose G=⟨a⟩G=\langle a\rangleG=⟨a⟩, ∣G∣=n|G|=n∣G∣=n.

Corollaries for subgroup of cyclic group

Non-cyclic groups

Let GGG be a group and a,b∈Ga,b\in Ga,b∈G, then we use ⟨a,b⟩\langle a,b\rangle⟨a,b⟩ to mean the subgroup of GGG generated by combination of aaa and bbb.

⟨a,b⟩≔{e,a,b,ab,ba,a−1,b−1,(ab)−1,(ba)−1,…}\langle a,b\rangle\coloneqq \{e,a,b,ab,ba,a^{-1},b^{-1},(ab)^{-1},(ba)^{-1},\ldots\}⟨a,b⟩:={e,a,b,ab,ba,a−1,b−1,(ab)−1,(ba)−1,…}

This is a subgroup of GGG since it is closed and e=a0e=a^0e=a0.

Definition of finitely generated group

If GGG is a group and if there is a finite set a1,…,an∈Ga_1,\ldots, a_n\in Ga1​,…,an​∈G such that G=⟨a1,…,an⟩G=\langle a_1,\ldots, a_n\rangleG=⟨a1​,…,an​⟩, then GGG is called finitely generated.

Examples

Any finite group is finitely generated.

(Q,+)(\mathbb{Q},+)(Q,+) is not finitely generated.

Suppose for the contrary, there is a finite set a1b1,…,anbn∈Q\frac{a_1}{b_1},\ldots,\frac{a_n}{b_n}\in \mathbb{Q}b1​a1​​,…,bn​an​​∈Q such that

\frac{1}{p}=t_1\frac{a_1}{b_1}+t_2\frac{a_2}{b_2}+\cdots+t_n\frac{a_n}{b_n}=\frac{A}{b_1b_2\cdots b_n}

This implies that $pA=b_1b_2\cdots b_n$. Since $p$ is prime, $p|b_i$ for some $i$. However, by our construction, $p>|b_i|$ and cannot divide $b_i$. Contradiction. </details>
