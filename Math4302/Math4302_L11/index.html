Symmetric groups

Definition of odd and even permutations

σ\sigmaσ is an even permutation if the number of transpositions is even.

σ\sigmaσ is an odd permutation if the number of transpositions is odd.

Theorem for parity of transpositions

The parity of the number of transpositions is unique.

Proof

Prove using the determinant of a matrix, swapping the rows of the matrix multiply the determinant by −1-1−1.

Consider the identity matrix InI_nIn​. Then the determinant is 111, let (ij)A(ij)A(ij)A, where i≠ji\neq ji=j denote the matrix obtained from AAA by swapping the rows jjj and iii, then the determinant of (1j)A(1j)A(1j)A is −1-1−1.

And,

det⁡((a1b1)(a2b2)⋯(anbn)A)=(−1)ndet⁡(A)\det((a_1b_1)(a_2b_2)\cdots(a_nb_n)A)=(-1)^n\det(A)det((a1​b1​)(a2​b2​)⋯(an​bn​)A)=(−1)ndet(A)

Theorem for the number of odd and even permutations in symmetric groups

In general, SnS_nSn​ has n!n!n! permutations, half of them are even and half of them are odd.

Proof

Consider the set of odd permutations in SnS_nSn​ and set of even permutations in SnS_nSn​. Consider the function: α:Sn→Sn\alpha:S_n\to S_nα:Sn​→Sn​ where α(σ)=σ(12)\alpha(\sigma)=\sigma(12)α(σ)=σ(12).

σ\sigmaσ is a bijection,

If σ1(12)=σ2(12)\sigma_1(12)=\sigma_2(12)σ1​(12)=σ2​(12), then σ1=σ2\sigma_1=\sigma_2σ1​=σ2​.

If ϕ\phiϕ is an even permutation, α(ϕ(12))=ϕ(12)(12)=ϕ\alpha(\phi(12))=\phi(12)(12)=\phiα(ϕ(12))=ϕ(12)(12)=ϕ, therefore the number of elements in the set of odd and even permutations are the same.

Definition for sign of permutations

For σ∈Sn\sigma\in S_nσ∈Sn​, the sign of σ\sigmaσ is defined by sign⁡(σ)=1\operatorname{sign}(\sigma)=1sign(σ)=1 if sigma is even and −1-1−1 if sigma is odd.

Then β:Sn→{1,−1}\beta: S_n\to \{1,-1\}β:Sn​→{1,−1} is a group under multiplication, where β(σ)=sign⁡(σ)\beta(\sigma)=\operatorname{sign}(\sigma)β(σ)=sign(σ).

Then β\betaβ is a group homomorphism.

Definition of alternating group

ker⁡(β)≤Sn\ker(\beta)\leq S_nker(β)≤Sn​, and ker⁡(β)\ker(\beta)ker(β) is the set of even permutations. Therefore the set of even permutations is a subgroup of SnS_nSn​. We denote as AnA_nAn​ (also called alternating group).

and ∣An∣=n!2|A_n|=\frac{n!}{2}∣An​∣=2n!​.

Definition of direct product of groups

Let G1,G2G_1,G_2G1​,G2​ be two groups. Then the direct product of G1G_1G1​ and G2G_2G2​ is defined as

G1×G2={(g1,g2):g1∈G1,g2∈G2}G_1\times G_2=\{(g_1,g_2):g_1\in G_1,g_2\in G_2\}G1​×G2​={(g1​,g2​):g1​∈G1​,g2​∈G2​}

The operations are defined by (a1,b1)∗(a2,b2)=(a1∗a2,b1∗b2)(a_1,b_1)*(a_2,b_2)=(a_1*a_2,b_1*b_2)(a1​,b1​)∗(a2​,b2​)=(a1​∗a2​,b1​∗b2​).

This group is well defined since:

The identity is (e1,e2)(e_1,e_2)(e1​,e2​), where e1∈G1e_1\in G_1e1​∈G1​ and e2∈G2e_2\in G_2e2​∈G2​. (easy to verify)

The inverse is (a1,b1)−1=(a1−1,b1−1)(a_1,b_1)^{-1}=(a_1^{-1},b_1^{-1})(a1​,b1​)−1=(a1−1​,b1−1​).

Associativity automatically holds by associativity of G1G_1G1​ and G2G_2G2​.

Lemma for direct product of cyclic groups

Zm×Zn≃Zmn\mathbb{Z}_m\times \mathbb{Z}_n\simeq \mathbb{Z}_{mn}Zm​×Zn​≃Zmn​ if and only if mmm and nnn have greatest common divisor 111.
