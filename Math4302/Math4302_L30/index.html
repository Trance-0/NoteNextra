Theorem for polynomial fields

If f(x),g(x)∈F[x]f(x),g(x)\in F[x]f(x),g(x)∈F[x], then there are unique q(x),r(x)∈F[x]q(x),r(x)\in F[x]q(x),r(x)∈F[x] such that f(x)=q(x)g(x)+r(x)f(x)=q(x)g(x)+r(x)f(x)=q(x)g(x)+r(x).

If r(x)=0r(x)=0r(x)=0, then g(x)∣f(x)g(x)|f(x)g(x)∣f(x).

  1. a∈Fa\in Fa∈F is a zero of f(x)  ⟺  x−a∣f(x)f(x)\iff x-a|f(x)f(x)⟺x−a∣f(x).

Irreducible polynomials

A non-zero polynomial f(x)∈F[x]f(x)\in F[x]f(x)∈F[x] is called irreducible if f(x)f(x)f(x) is not the product of two non-zero polynomials in F[x]F[x]F[x]. (f(x)=g(x)h(x)f(x)=g(x)h(x)f(x)=g(x)h(x), with deg⁡g(x),deg⁡h(x)<deg⁡f(x)\deg g(x),\deg h(x)<\deg f(x)degg(x),degh(x)<degf(x))

So non-zero constant polynomials are always irreducible.

A polynomial f(x)∈F[x]f(x)\in F[x]f(x)∈F[x] of deg⁡f(x)>1\deg f(x)>1degf(x)>1 is called reducible if it is not irreducible.

Polynomials of degree 111 are always irreducible.

Polynomials of degree 222 is reducible if and only if fff haz zero in FFF.
