Field of quotients

Let RRR be an integral domain (RRR has unity and commutative with no zero divisors).

Consider the pair S={(a,b)∣a,b∈R,b≠0}S=\{(a,b)|a,b\in R, b\neq 0\}S={(a,b)∣a,b∈R,b=0}.

(a,b)∼(c,d)(a,b)\sim (c,d)(a,b)∼(c,d) if and only if ad=bcad=bcad=bc.

Claim (F,+,*) is a field

  • additive identity: (0,1)∈F(0,1)\in F(0,1)∈F

  • additive inverse: (a,b)∈F(a,b)\in F(a,b)∈F, then (−a,b)∈F(-a,b)\in F(−a,b)∈F and (−a,b)+(a,b)=(0,1)∈F(-a,b)+(a,b)=(0,1)\in F(−a,b)+(a,b)=(0,1)∈F

  • additive associativity: bit long.

  • multiplicative identity: (1,1)∈F(1,1)\in F(1,1)∈F

  • multiplicative associativity: bit long
