Groups

Basic definitions

Definition for group

A group is a set GGG with a binary operation ∗*∗ that satisfies the following axioms:

  1. Closure: ∀a,b∈G,a∗b∈G\forall a,b\in G, a* b\in G∀a,b∈G,a∗b∈G (automatically guaranteed by definition of binary operation).
  2. Associativity: ∀a,b,c∈G,(a∗b)∗c=a∗(b∗c)\forall a,b,c\in G, (a* b)* c=a* (b* c)∀a,b,c∈G,(a∗b)∗c=a∗(b∗c).
  3. Identity: ∃e∈G,∀a∈G,e∗a=a∗e=a\exists e\in G, \forall a\in G, e* a=a* e=a∃e∈G,∀a∈G,e∗a=a∗e=a.
  4. Inverses: ∀a∈G,∃a−1∈G,a∗a−1=a−1∗a=e\forall a\in G, \exists a^{-1}\in G, a* a^{-1}=a^{-1}* a=e∀a∈G,∃a−1∈G,a∗a−1=a−1∗a=e.
  • Identity element: If XXX has an identity element, then it is unique.
  • Composition of function is associative.

Order of a element

The order of an element aaa in a group GGG is the size of the smallest subgroup generated by aaa, we denote such subgroup as ⟨a⟩\langle a\rangle⟨a⟩.

Equivalently, the order of aaa is the smallest positive integer nnn such that an=ea^n=ean=e.

Order of a group

The order of a group GGG is the size of GGG.

Definition of subgroup

A subgroup HHH of a group GGG is a subset of GGG that is closed under the group operation. Denoted as H≤GH\leq GH≤G.

If HHH is a subgroup of GGG, then aHaHaH is a coset of HHH for all a∈Ga\in Ga∈G. We call aHaHaH a left coset of HHH for aaa.

Definition of normal subgroup

A subgroup HHH of a group GGG is normal if aH=HaaH=HaaH=Ha for all a∈Ga\in Ga∈G.

Isomorphism and homomorphism

Definition of isomorphism

Two groups GGG and G′G'G′ are isomorphic if there exists a function f:G→G′f:G\to G'f:G→G′ such that

  • Homomorphism property is satisfied: f(a∗b)=f(a)f(b),∀a,b∈Gf(a*b)=f(a)f(b),\forall a,b\in Gf(a∗b)=f(a)f(b),∀a,b∈G
  • fff is injective: f(a)=f(b)  ⟹  a=bf(a)=f(b)\implies a=bf(a)=f(b)⟹a=b
  • fff is surjective: ∀a∈G′,∃b∈G\forall a\in G',\exists b\in G∀a∈G′,∃b∈G such that f(b)=af(b)=af(b)=a

A homomorphism is a function that satisfies the homomorphism property.

If ϕ:G→G′\phi:G\to G'ϕ:G→G′ is a homomorphism, then

  • ϕ(e)=e′\phi(e)=e'ϕ(e)=e′, where eee is the identity of GGG and e′e'e′ is the identity of G′G'G′.
  • ϕ(a−1)=(ϕ(a))−1\phi(a^{-1})=(\phi(a))^{-1}ϕ(a−1)=(ϕ(a))−1 for all a∈Ga\in Ga∈G.
  • If H≤GH\leq GH≤G is a subgroup, then ϕ(H)≤G′\phi(H)\leq G'ϕ(H)≤G′ is a subgroup.
  • If K≤G′K\leq G'K≤G′ is a subgroup, then ϕ−1(K)≤G\phi^{-1}(K)\leq Gϕ−1(K)≤G is a subgroup.
  • ϕ\phiϕ is surjective if and only if ker⁡(ϕ)={e}\operatorname{ker}(\phi)=\{e\}ker(ϕ)={e} (the trivial subgroup of GGG).

Basic groups

Trivial group

The group ({e},∗)(\{e\},*)({e},∗) is called the trivial group.

Abelian group

A group GGG is abelian if a∗b=b∗aa*b=b*aa∗b=b∗a for all a,b∈Ga,b\in Ga,b∈G.

  • The smallest non-abelian group is S3S_3S3​ (order 6).
  • Every abelian group is isomorphic to some direct product of cyclic groups of the form: Zp1n1×Zp2n2×⋯×Zpknk×Z×…×Z⏟m times\mathbb{Z}_{p_1^{n_1}}\times \mathbb{Z}_{p_2^{n_2}}\times \cdots \times \mathbb{Z}_{p_k^{n_k}}\times\underbrace{\mathbb{Z}\times \ldots \times \mathbb{Z}}_{m\text{ times}}Zp1n1​​​×Zp2n2​​​×⋯×Zpknk​​​×m timesZ×…×Z​​

Cyclic group

A group GGG is cyclic if GGG is a subgroup generated by a∈Ga\in Ga∈G. (may be infinite)

  • The smallest non-cyclic group is Klein 4-group (order 4).
  • Every group with prime order is cyclic.
  • Every cyclic group is abelian.
  • If GGG has order nnn, then GGG is isomorphic to (Zn,+)(\mathbb{Z}_n,+)(Zn​,+).
  • If GGG is infinite, then GGG is isomorphic to (Z,+)(\mathbb{Z},+)(Z,+).
  • If G=⟨a⟩G=\langle a\rangleG=⟨a⟩ and H=⟨ak⟩H=\langle a^k\rangleH=⟨ak⟩, then ∣H∣=∣G∣d|H|=\frac{|G|}{d}∣H∣=d∣G∣​ where d=gcd⁡(∣G∣,∣H∣)d=\operatorname{gcd}(|G|,|H|)d=gcd(∣G∣,∣H∣).
  • Every subgroup of cyclic group is also cyclic.

Dihedral group

The dihedral group DnD_nDn​ is the group of all symmetries of a regular polygon with nnn sides.

  • ∣Dn∣=2n|D_n|=2n∣Dn​∣=2n.
  • It is finitely generated by {ρ,ϕ}\{\rho,\phi\}{ρ,ϕ}, where ρ\rhoρ is a rotation of a regular polygon by 2πn\frac{2\pi}{n}n2π​, and ϕ\phiϕ is a reflection of a regular polygon with respect to xxx-axis.

The symmetric group SnS_nSn​ is the group of all permutations of nnn objects.
