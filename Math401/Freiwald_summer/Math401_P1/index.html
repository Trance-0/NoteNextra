Quantum capacity for a quantum channel

The quantum capacity of a quantum channel is governed by the HSW noisy coding theorem, which is the counterpart for the Shannon’s noisy coding theorem in quantum information settings.

Superdense coding of quantum states

It is a procedure defined as follows:

Suppose AAA and BBB share a Bell state ∣Φ+⟩=12(∣00⟩+∣11⟩)|\Phi^+\rangle=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(|00\rangle+|11\rangle)∣Φ+⟩=2​1​(∣00⟩+∣11⟩), where AAA holds the first part and BBB holds the second part.

AAA wish to send 2 classical bits to BBB.

AAA performs one of four Pauli unitaries on the combined state of entangled qubits ⊗\otimes⊗ one qubit. Then AAA sends the resulting one qubit to BBB.

This operation extends the initial one entangled qubit to a system of one of four orthogonal Bell states.

BBB performs a measurement on the combined state of the one qubit and the entangled qubits he holds.

BBB decodes the result and obtains the 2 classical bits sent by AAA.
