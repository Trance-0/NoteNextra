Drawing the connection between the space S2n+1S^{2n+1}S2n+1, CPnCP^nCPn, and R\mathbb{R}R

A pure quantum state of size NNN can be identified with a Hopf circle on the sphere S2N−1S^{2N-1}S2N−1.

A random pure state ∣ψ⟩|\psi\rangle∣ψ⟩ of a bipartite N×KN\times KN×K system such that K≥N≥3K\geq N\geq 3K≥N≥3.

The partial trace of such system produces a mixed state ρ(ψ)=Tr⁡K(∣ψ⟩⟨ψ∣)\rho(\psi)=\operatorname{Tr}_K(|\psi\rangle\langle \psi|)ρ(ψ)=TrK​(∣ψ⟩⟨ψ∣), with induced measure μK\mu_KμK​. When K=NK=NK=N, the induced measure μK\mu_KμK​ is the Hilbert-Schmidt measure.

Consider the function f:S2N−1→Rf:S^{2N-1}\to \mathbb{R}f:S2N−1→R defined by f(x)=S(ρ(ψ))f(x)=S(\rho(\psi))f(x)=S(ρ(ψ)), where S(⋅)S(\cdot)S(⋅) is the von Neumann entropy. The Lipschitz constant of fff is ∼ln⁡N\sim \ln N∼lnN.
