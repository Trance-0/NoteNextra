Definition 7.1

Suppose T∈L(V,W)T\in \mathscr{L}(V,W)T∈L(V,W). The adjoint is the function T∗:W→VT^*:W\to VT∗:W→V such that

⟨Tv,w⟩=⟨v,T∗w⟩,∀v∈V,w∈W\langle Tv,w \rangle=\langle v,T^*w \rangle, \forall v\in V, w\in W⟨Tv,w⟩=⟨v,T∗w⟩,∀v∈V,w∈W

⟨v,T∗(w1+w2)⟩=⟨v,T∗(w1+w2)⟩=⟨Tv,w1+w2⟩=⟨Tv,w1⟩+⟨Tv,w2⟩=⟨v,T∗w1⟩+⟨v,T∗w2⟩=⟨v,T∗w1+T∗w2⟩\begin{aligned} \langle v,T^*(w_1+w_2) \rangle &=\langle v,T^*(w_1+w_2) \rangle\\ &=\langle Tv,w_1+w_2 \rangle\\ &=\langle Tv,w_1 \rangle+\langle Tv,w_2 \rangle\\ &=\langle v,T^*w_1 \rangle+\langle v,T^* w_2 \rangle\\ &=\langle v,T^*w_1 +T^* w_2 \rangle\\ \end{aligned}⟨v,T∗(w1​+w2​)⟩​=⟨v,T∗(w1​+w2​)⟩=⟨Tv,w1​+w2​⟩=⟨Tv,w1​⟩+⟨Tv,w2​⟩=⟨v,T∗w1​⟩+⟨v,T∗w2​⟩=⟨v,T∗w1​+T∗w2​⟩​
