Recap

Group

  1. Closure: ∀a,b∈G,a⋅b∈G\forall a,b\in G, a\cdot b\in G∀a,b∈G,a⋅b∈G.
  2. Associativity: ∀a,b,c∈G,(a⋅b)⋅c=a⋅(b⋅c)\forall a,b,c\in G, (a\cdot b)\cdot c=a\cdot (b\cdot c)∀a,b,c∈G,(a⋅b)⋅c=a⋅(b⋅c).
  3. Identity: ∃e∈G,∀a∈G,a⋅e=e⋅a=a\exists e\in G, \forall a\in G, a\cdot e=e\cdot a=a∃e∈G,∀a∈G,a⋅e=e⋅a=a.
  4. Inverses: ∀a∈G,∃a−1∈G,a⋅a−1=a−1⋅a=e\forall a\in G, \exists a^{-1}\in G, a\cdot a^{-1}=a^{-1}\cdot a=e∀a∈G,∃a−1∈G,a⋅a−1=a−1⋅a=e.

May not be commutative (group of invertible matrices).

Order of element in group

a∈Ga\in Ga∈G is of order kkk if ak=ea^k=eak=e and kkk is the smallest positive integer such that ak=ea^k=eak=e.

If an=ea^n=ean=e, then O(a)∣nO(a)\mid nO(a)∣n.

Generator of group

a∈Ga\in Ga∈G is a generator of GGG if O(a)=∣G∣\mathcal{O}(a)=|G|O(a)=∣G∣. (for finite groups)

For infinite groups, ⟨a⟩=G\langle a\rangle=G⟨a⟩=G.

Example

(Zn,+)(\mathbb{Z}_n,+)(Zn​,+) has generator 111.

(Z8∗,⋅)(\mathbb{Z}_8^*,\cdot)(Z8∗​,⋅) has generator 333. (Recall Z8∗={x∈Z8:gcd(x,8)=1}\mathbb{Z}_8^*=\{x\in\mathbb{Z}_8:gcd(x,8)=1\}Z8∗​={x∈Z8​:gcd(x,8)=1}. for multiplicative inverse.)

Subgroups

A subgroup HHH of GGG is a nonempty subset of GGG that is itself a group under the operation of GGG.

Denoted as H≤GH\leq GH≤G.

Example

(Z6,+)(\mathbb{Z}_6,+)(Z6​,+) has subgroups H=({0,2,4},+)H=(\{0,2,4\},+)H=({0,2,4},+).

Only need to check three:

  • non-empty
  • closure
  • finite

Theorem for finite subgroups

If HHH is finite, non-empty, and closed under the operation of GGG, then HHH is a subgroup of GGG.

Equivalence relations

An equivalence relation ∼\sim∼ on a set XXX is a relation that is

  • reflexive: ∀x∈X,x∼x\forall x\in X, x\sim x∀x∈X,x∼x
  • symmetric: ∀x,y∈X,x∼y  ⟹  y∼x\forall x,y\in X, x\sim y\implies y\sim x∀x,y∈X,x∼y⟹y∼x
  • transitive: ∀x,y,z∈X,x∼y and y∼z  ⟹  x∼z\forall x,y,z\in X, x\sim y\text{ and } y\sim z\implies x\sim z∀x,y,z∈X,x∼y and y∼z⟹x∼z

Example

Let SSS be points on land, and a∼ba\sim ba∼b if aaa and bbb are connected by land.

An equivalence relation on SSS partitions SSS into equivalence classes.

Equivalence classes are:

  • Disjoint
  • Cover SSS

Cosets

Let GGG be a group and HHH its subgroup.

The coset of HHH in GGG is the equivalence class under congruence modulo HHH.

Alternatively, (more convenient)

{h+a∣h∈H,a∈G}\{h+a|h\in H,a\in G\}{h+a∣h∈H,a∈G}

a∼ba\sim ba∼b if a+b−1∈Ha+b^{-1}\in Ha+b−1∈H (congruence modulo HHH)

Lagrange’s theorem

For every finite group GGG, the order of every subgroup HHH of GGG divides the order of GGG.

Corollary of Lagrange’s theorem

If ∣G∣|G|∣G∣ is prime, then GGG is cyclic.

Proof

Let A={a0=e,a,a2,⋯ ,a∣G∣−1}A=\{a^0=e,a,a^2,\cdots,a^{|G|-1}\}A={a0=e,a,a2,⋯,a∣G∣−1}.

AAA is a cyclic subgroup of GGG. of order at least two.

Then ∣A∣∣∣G∣|A|||G|∣A∣∣∣G∣.

So ∣A∣=∣G∣|A|=|G|∣A∣=∣G∣.

So A=GA=GA=G.

So GGG is cyclic.

Additive group of a field

Any finite field has two types groups:

  • Additive group: (F,+)(\mathbb{F},+)(F,+)
  • Multiplicative group: (F∗,⋅)(\mathbb{F}^*,\cdot)(F∗,⋅)

The “integer” of FFF is:

{a∈F∣1k=a,k∈N}\{a\in F|1^k=a,k\in\mathbb{N}\}{a∈F∣1k=a,k∈N}

The “characteristic” of FFF is:

  • The order of 111 in additive group
  • Number of times that 111 is added to itself to get 000
  • Denoted by c⁡(F)\operatorname{c}(F)c(F).

Example

c⁡(Z7)=7\operatorname{c}(\mathbb{Z}_7)=7c(Z7​)=7.

c⁡(R)=0\operatorname{c}(\mathbb{R})=0c(R)=0.

c⁡(Z2[x]mod  x2+x+1)=2\operatorname{c}(\mathbb{Z}_2[x] \mod x^2+x+1)=2c(Z2​[x]modx2+x+1)=2.

Theorem field characteristic is prime

If c⁡(F)>0\operatorname{c}(F)>0c(F)>0, then c⁡(F)\operatorname{c}(F)c(F) is prime.

Proof

Suppose c⁡(F)=mn\operatorname{c}(F)=mnc(F)=mn, then 0=∑i=0m−11⋅∑j=0n−11=00=\sum_{i=0}^{m-1}1\cdot\sum_{j=0}^{n-1}1=00=∑i=0m−1​1⋅∑j=0n−1​1=0.

So mmm or nnn must be 000.

So c⁡(F)\operatorname{c}(F)c(F) is prime.

Multiplicative group of a field

Every element in a multiplicative group of a field is cyclic.

Corollary:

  • Every finite field has a generator, called a primitive element.
  • This is an element γ\gammaγ such that F∗=⟨γ⟩\mathbb{F}^*=\langle \gamma\rangleF∗=⟨γ⟩.
  • Every element of F∗\mathbb{F}^*F∗ is a power of γ\gammaγ.
