Structure from Motion

Not always uniquely solvable.

If we scale the entire scene by some factor kkk and, at the same time, scale the camera matrices by the factor of 1/k1/k1/k, the projections of the scene points remain exactly the same: x≅PX=(1/kP)(kX)x\cong PX =(1/k P)(kX)x≅PX=(1/kP)(kX)

Without a reference measurement, it is impossible to recover the absolute scale of the scene!

In general, if we transform the scene using a transformation QQQ and apply the inverse transformation to the camera matrices, then the image observations do not change:

x≅PX=(PQ−1)(QX)x\cong PX =(P Q^{-1})(QX)x≅PX=(PQ−1)(QX)
