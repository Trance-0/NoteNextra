Possible Cases for the Convergence of Power Series

  1. Convergence Only at z=0z = 0z=0:

    • Proof: If the power series ∑n=0∞cn(z−z0)n\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n (z - z_0)^n∑n=0∞​cn​(z−z0​)n converges only at z=0z = 0z=0, it means that the radius of convergence R=0R = 0R=0.   2. Convergence Everywhere:

This implies that the terms cn(z−z0)nc_n (z - z_0)^ncn​(z−z0​)n tend to zero for all zzz. This can happen if the coefficients cnc_ncn​ decrease rapidly enough, such as in the exponential series.
