Simple connectedness

Definition (non-standard) simply connected

Let Ω\OmegaΩ be a domain in C\mathbb{C}C. We say Ω\OmegaΩ is simply connected if C‾∖Ω\overline{\mathbb{C}}\setminus \OmegaC∖Ω is connected. (C‾=C∪{∞})\overline{\mathbb{C}}=\mathbb{C}\cup \{\infty\})C=C∪{∞})

Example:

disk is simply connected.

annulus is not simply connected.

C\mathbb{C}C is simply connected.

Any convex domain is simply connected.

Standard definition: Ω\OmegaΩ is simply connected if every closed curve in Ω\OmegaΩ is null-homotopic in Ω\OmegaΩ.

Theorem of equivalent definition of simply connected

For open connected subsets of C\mathbb{C}C, the standard definition and the non-standard definition are equivalent.
