Theorem L.3 (Dominated Convergence Theorem)

Let {fn}n=1∞\{f_n\}_{n=1}^\infty{fn​}n=1∞​ be a sequence of measurable functions on R\mathbb{R}R converging to fff almost everywhere. If there exists integrable ggg such that ∣fn∣≤∣g∣|f_n|\leq |g|∣fn​∣≤∣g∣ for all nnn, then

Proof:

Consider the function g+fng+f_ng+fn​ and g−fng-f_ng−fn​, these are non-negative sequences of measurable functions. By Fatou’s lemma, we have

∫g dm+∫f dm=∫Elim inf⁡n→∞(g+fn) dm≤lim inf⁡n→∞∫E(g+fn) dm=∫Eg dm+lim inf⁡n→∞∫Efn dm\int g\,dm+\int f\,dm=\int_E \liminf_{n\to\infty} (g+f_n) \, dm\leq \liminf_{n\to\infty} \int_E (g+f_n) \, dm=\int_E g\,dm+\liminf_{n\to\infty} \int_E f_n\,dm∫gdm+∫fdm=∫E​n→∞liminf​(g+fn​)dm≤n→∞liminf​∫E​(g+fn​)dm=∫E​gdm+n→∞liminf​∫E​fn​dm

So, ∫Ef dm≤lim inf⁡n→∞∫Efn dm\int_E f\,dm\leq \liminf_{n\to\infty} \int_E f_n\,dm∫E​fdm≤liminfn→∞​∫E​fn​dm.

Similarly, we have

∫g dm−∫f dm=∫Elim inf⁡n→∞(g−fn) dm≤lim inf⁡n→∞∫E(g−fn) dm=∫Eg dm−lim sup⁡n→∞∫Efn dm\int g\,dm-\int f\,dm=\int_E \liminf_{n\to\infty} (g-f_n) \, dm\leq \liminf_{n\to\infty} \int_E (g-f_n) \, dm=\int_E g\,dm-\limsup_{n\to\infty} \int_E f_n\,dm∫gdm−∫fdm=∫E​n→∞liminf​(g−fn​)dm≤n→∞liminf​∫E​(g−fn​)dm=∫E​gdm−n→∞limsup​∫E​fn​dm

So, lim sup⁡n→∞∫Efn dm≤∫Ef dm\limsup_{n\to\infty} \int_E f_n\,dm\leq \int_E f\,dmlimsupn→∞​∫E​fn​dm≤∫E​fdm.

So lim sup⁡n→∞∫Efn dm≤∫Ef dm≤lim inf⁡n→∞∫Efn dm\limsup_{n\to\infty} \int_E f_n\,dm\leq \int_E f\,dm\leq \liminf_{n\to\infty} \int_E f_n\,dmlimsupn→∞​∫E​fn​dm≤∫E​fdm≤liminfn→∞​∫E​fn​dm.

Since lim sup⁡n→∞∫Efn dm≥lim inf⁡n→∞∫Efn dm\limsup_{n\to\infty} \int_E f_n\,dm\geq \liminf_{n\to\infty} \int_E f_n\,dmlimsupn→∞​∫E​fn​dm≥liminfn→∞​∫E​fn​dm, we have ∫Ef dm=lim⁡n→∞∫Efn dm\int_E f\,dm=\lim_{n\to\infty} \int_E f_n\,dm∫E​fdm=limn→∞​∫E​fn​dm.
