LUBP (The least upper bound property)

Proof that LUBP  ⟹  GLBPLUBP\implies GLBPLUBP⟹GLBP.

Proof

Let SSS be an ordered set with LUBP. Let B<SB<SB<S be non-empty and bounded below.

Let L=y∈S:yL=y\in S:yL=y∈S:y is a lower bound of BBB. From the picture, we expect sup⁡L=inf⁡B\sup L=\inf BsupL=infB First we’ll show sup⁡L\sup LsupL exists.

  1. To show L≠ϕL\neq \phiL=ϕ.

    BBB is bounded below   ⟹  L≠ϕ\implies L\neq\phi⟹L=ϕ

  2. To show LLL id bounded above.

    BBB is not empty   ⟹  ∃x∈B  ⟹  x\implies \exists x\in B\implies x⟹∃x∈B⟹x is a upper bound of LLL.

  3. Since SSS has the least upper bound property, sup⁡L\sup LsupL exists (in SSS).

Let’s say α=sup L\alpha=sup\ Lα=sup L. We claim that α=inf B\alpha=inf\ Bα=inf B. We need to show 222 things.

  1. To show α\alphaα is a lower bound of BBB, ∀γ∈B,α≤γ\forall \gamma\in B,\alpha\leq \gamma∀γ∈B,α≤γ.

    Let γ∈B\gamma\in Bγ∈B, then γ\gammaγ is an upper bound of LLL.

    Since α\alphaα is the least upper bound of LLL, α≤γ\alpha\leq \gammaα≤γ.

  2. To show α\alphaα is the greatest lower bound of BBB, ∀β>α,β\forall \beta>\alpha,\beta∀β>α,β is not a lower bound of BBB.

    Let β>α\beta>\alphaβ>α. Since α\alphaα is an upper bound of LLL, β∉L\beta\notin Lβ∈/L.

    By definition of LLL, β\betaβ is not a lower bound of BBB.

Thus α=inf B\alpha=inf\ Bα=inf B
