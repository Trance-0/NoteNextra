Separation Axioms

Regular spaces

Proposition for T1T_1T1​ spaces

If XXX is a topological space such that all singleton are closed, then then following holds:

  • XXX is regular if and only if for any point x∈Xx\in Xx∈X and any open neighborhood VVV of XXX such that V‾⊆U\overline{V}\subseteq UV⊆U.
  • XXX is normal if and only if for any closed set A⊆XA\subseteq XA⊆X, there is an open neighborhood VVV of AAA such that V‾⊆U\overline{V}\subseteq UV⊆U.

Proposition of regular and Hausdorff on subspaces

  1. If XXX is a regular topological space, and YYY is a subspace. Then YYY with induced topology is regular. (same holds for Hausdorff)
  2. If {Xα}\{X_\alpha\}{Xα​} is a collection of regular topological spaces, then their product with the product topology is regular. (same holds for Hausdorff)

  1. If XXX is regular and Y⊆XY\subseteq XY⊆X,then we want to show that YYY is regular.

We take a point y∈Yy\in Yy∈Y and a closed subset AAA of YYY which doesn’t contain yyy.

Observe that A⊆YA\subseteq YA⊆Y is closed if and only if A=A‾∩YA=\overline{A}\cap YA=A∩Y where A‾\overline{A}A is the closure of AAA in XXX.

Notice that y∈Yy\in Yy∈Y but y∉Ay\notin Ay∈/A, then y∉A‾y\notin \overline{A}y∈/A (otherwise y∈Ay\in Ay∈A).

By regularity of XXX, we can find an open neighborhood y∈U⊆Xy\in U\subseteq Xy∈U⊆X and A‾⊆V⊆X\overline{A}\subseteq V\subseteq XA⊆V⊆X and U,VU,VU,V are open and disjoint.

This implies that U∩YU\cap YU∩Y and V∩YV\cap YV∩Y are open neighborhood of yyy and AAA and disjoint from each other.

How we construct new normal spaces from existing one

Theorem for constructing normal spaces

  1. Any compact Hausdorff space is normal
  2. Any regular second countable space is normal

Proof

For the first proposition

Earlier we showed that any compact Hausdorff space XXX is regular, i.e., for any closed subspace AAA of XXX and a point x∈Xx\in Xx∈X not in AAA. There are open neighborhoods UxU_xUx​ of AAA and VxV_xVx​ of xxx such that Ux∩Vx=∅U_x\cap V_x=\emptysetUx​∩Vx​=∅.

Now let BBB be a closed subset of XXX disjoint from AAA.

For any x∈Bx\in Bx∈B, we know that we have a disjoint open neighborhood UxU_xUx​ of AAA and VxV_xVx​ of xxx.

{Vn}n∈B\{V_n\}_{n\in \mathbb{B}}{Vn​}n∈B​ gives an open covering of BBB, BBB is closed subset of a compact space, therefore, BBB is compact.

This implies that ∃x1,x2,…,xn\exists x_1,x_2,\ldots,x_n∃x1​,x2​,…,xn​ such that B⊆⋃i=1nVxiB\subseteq \bigcup_{i=1}^n V_{x_i}B⊆⋃i=1n​Vxi​​.

In summary, UUU and VVV are disjoint open neighborhoods of AAA and BBB respectively.
