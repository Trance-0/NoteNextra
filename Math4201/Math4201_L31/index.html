Compactness

Local compactness

R\mathbb{R}R is not compact but it has a “lot” of compact subspaces.

An arbitrary point x∈Rx\in\mathbb{R}x∈R then there is a subset (x−ϵ,x+ϵ)U⊆R(x-\epsilon,x+\epsilon)U\subseteq \mathbb{R}(x−ϵ,x+ϵ)U⊆R such that x∈Ux\in Ux∈U and UUU is compact.

Definition of local compactness

A space XXX is locally compact if every point x∈Xx\in Xx∈X, there is a compact subspace KKK of XXX containing a neighborhood UUU of xxx x∈U⊆Kx\in U\subseteq Kx∈U⊆K such that KKK is compact.

Theorem of Homeomorphism over locally compact Hausdorff spaces

XXX is a locally compact Hausdorff space if and only if there exists topological space YYY satisfying the following properties:

  1. XXX is a subspace of YYY.
  2. Y−XY-XY−X has one point (usually denoted by ∞\infty∞).
  3. YYY is compact and Hausdorff.

YYY is unique in the following sense:

If Y′Y'Y′ is another such space, then there is a homeomorphism between YYY and Y′Y'Y′ f(x)=xf(x)=xf(x)=x for any x∈Xx\in Xx∈X.
