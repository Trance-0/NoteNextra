Separation Axioms

Embedding manifolds

A ddd dimensional manifold is the topological space satisfying the following three properties:

  1. Haudorff property (∀x,y∈X,∃U,V∈TX\forall x,y\in X, \exists U,V\in \mathcal{T}_X∀x,y∈X,∃U,V∈TX​ such that x∈U∩Vx\in U\cap Vx∈U∩V and y∉U∩Vy\notin U\cap Vy∈/U∩V)
  2. Second countable property (∃B⊆TX\exists \mathcal{B}\subseteq \mathcal{T}_X∃B⊆TX​ such that B\mathcal{B}B is a basis for XXX and B\mathcal{B}B is countable)
  3. Local homeomorphism to Rd\mathbb{R}^dRd (∀x∈M\forall x\in M∀x∈M, there is a neighborhood UUU of xxx such that UUU is homeomorphic to Rd\mathbb{R}^dRd. φ:U→Rd\varphi:U\to \mathbb{R}^dφ:U→Rd is bijective, continuous, and open)

Example of manifold

Rd\mathbb{R}^dRd is a ddd-dimensional manifold. And any open subspace of Rd\mathbb{R}^dRd is also a manifold.

S1S^1S1 is a 111-dimensional manifold.

T=R2/Z2T=\mathbb{R}^2/\mathbb{Z}^2T=R2/Z2 is a 222-dimensional manifold.

Recall the Urysohn metirzation theorem. Any normal and second countable space is metrizable.

In the proof we saw that any such space can be embedded into Rω\mathbb{R}^\omegaRω with the product topology.

Question: What topological space can be embedded into Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn with the product topology?

Theorem for embedding compact manifolds into Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn

Any ddd-dimensional (compact, this assumption makes the proof easier) manifold can be embedded into Rn\mathbb{R}^nRn with the product topology.

Definition for partition of unity

  2. ∑i=1nϕi(x)=1\sum_{i=1}^n \phi_i(x)=1∑i=1n​ϕi​(x)=1 for all x∈Xx\in Xx∈X

Theorem for existence of partition of unity

Let XXX be a normal space and {Ui}i=1n\{U_i\}_{i=1}^n{Ui​}i=1n​ is an open covering of XXX. Then there is a partition of unity dominated by {Ui}i=1n\{U_i\}_{i=1}^n{Ui​}i=1n​.

Proof uses Urysohn’s lemma.

Proof for embedding compact manifolds

Let MMM be a compact manifold.

For any point x∈Mx\in Mx∈M, there is an open neighborhood UxU_xUx​ of xxx such that UxU_xUx​ is homeomorphic to Rd\mathbb{R}^dRd.

Let {Ux}x∈M\{U_x\}_{x\in M}{Ux​}x∈M​ be an open cover of MMM.

Since MMM is compact, {Ux}x∈M\{U_x\}_{x\in M}{Ux​}x∈M​ has a finite subcover.

then {Uxi}i=1n\{U_{x_i}\}_{i=1}^n{Uxi​​}i=1n​ is an open cover of MMM.

Therefore Fi:Uxi→RdF_i:U_{x_i}\to \mathbb{R}^dFi​:Uxi​​→Rd is a homeomorphism.

Since MMM is compact and second countable, MMM is normal.
