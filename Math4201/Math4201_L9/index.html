Convergence of sequences

Let (X,T)(X,\mathcal{T})(X,T) be a topological space and {xn}n∈N+\{x_n\}_{n\in\mathbb{N}_+}{xn​}n∈N+​​ be a sequence of points in XXX. We say xn→xx_n\to xxn​→x as n→∞n\to \inftyn→∞ (xnx_nxn​ converges to xxx as n→∞n\to \inftyn→∞)

if for any open neighborhood UUU of xxx, there exists N∈N+N\in\mathbb{N}_+N∈N+​ such that ∀n≥N,xn∈U\forall n\geq N, x_n\in U∀n≥N,xn​∈U.

Example of convergence of sequences

Let X={a,b,c}X=\{a,b,c\}X={a,b,c} with the topology T={∅,{b},{a,b},{b,c},{a,b,c}}\mathcal{T}=\{\emptyset, \{b\}, \{a,b\}, \{b,c\}, \{a,b,c\}\}T={∅,{b},{a,b},{b,c},{a,b,c}}

Let xn=bx_n=bxn​=b for all n∈N+n\in\mathbb{N}_+n∈N+​. Then xn→bx_n\to bxn​→b as n→∞n\to \inftyn→∞.

Moreover, xn→ax_n\to axn​→a as n→∞n\to \inftyn→∞ since any open neighborhood of aaa ({a,b}\{a,b\}{a,b},{a,b,c}\{a,b,c\}{a,b,c}) contains bbb.

Without loss of generality, xn→cx_n\to cxn​→c as n→∞n\to \inftyn→∞ since any open neighborhood of ccc ({b,c}\{b,c\}{b,c},{a,b,c}\{a,b,c\}{a,b,c}) contains bbb.

You may find convergence of sequences in more than one point.

Let xn=ax_n=axn​=a for all n∈N+n\in\mathbb{N}_+n∈N+​. Then xn→ax_n\to axn​→a as n→∞n\to \inftyn→∞ (take U={a,b}U=\{a,b\}U={a,b})

A non-example of convergence of sequences:

Let xn={a,n is evenc,n is oddx_n=\begin{cases}a, & n\text{ is even} \\ c, & n\text{ is odd}\end{cases}xn​={a,c,​n is evenn is odd​. Then xnx_nxn​ does not converge to any point in XXX. So this sequence does not have a limit in (X,T)(X,\mathcal{T})(X,T).

More special topologies

Hausdorff space

A topological space (X,T)(X,\mathcal{T})(X,T) is a Hausdorff space if for any two distinct points x,y∈Xx,y\in Xx,y∈X, there exist open neighborhoods UUU and VVV of xxx and yyy respectively such that U∩V=∅U\cap V=\emptysetU∩V=∅.

Non-example of Hausdorff space

Let X={a,b,c}X=\{a,b,c\}X={a,b,c} with the topology T={∅,{b},{a,b},{b,c},{a,b,c}}\mathcal{T}=\{\emptyset, \{b\}, \{a,b\}, \{b,c\}, \{a,b,c\}\}T={∅,{b},{a,b},{b,c},{a,b,c}}

Let x=a,y=bx=a,y=bx=a,y=b. Then they don’t have disjoint open neighborhoods.

This topology is not a Hausdorff space.

If a topological space is a Hausdorff space, then every sequence has a unique limit point or have no limit point.

Properties of Hausdorff space

Let (X,T)(X,\mathcal{T})(X,T) be a Hausdorff space. Then every sequence {xn}n∈N+\{x_n\}_{n\in\mathbb{N}_+}{xn​}n∈N+​​ converges to xxx and yyy, then x=yx=yx=y.

Tip

We want to show if x≠yx\neq yx=y, then there exists an open neighborhood UUU of xxx and VVV of yyy such that U∩V=∅U\cap V=\emptysetU∩V=∅.

Proof

We proceed by contradiction.

Suppose x≠yx\neq yx=y, then by definition of Hausdorff space, there exists an open neighborhood UUU of xxx and VVV of yyy such that U∩V=∅U\cap V=\emptysetU∩V=∅.

If xnx_nxn​ converges to xxx, then for any open neighborhood UxU_xUx​ of xxx, there exists Nx∈N+N_x\in\mathbb{N}_+Nx​∈N+​ such that ∀n≥Nx,xn∈Ux\forall n\geq N_x, x_n\in U_x∀n≥Nx​,xn​∈Ux​. Similarly, for any open neighborhood UyU_yUy​ of yyy, there exists Ny∈N+N_y\in\mathbb{N}_+Ny​∈N+​ such that ∀n≥Ny,xn∈Uy\forall n\geq N_y, x_n\in U_y∀n≥Ny​,xn​∈Uy​.

Then we can find N=max{Nx,Ny}N=max\{N_x,N_y\}N=max{Nx​,Ny​} such that xn∈Ux∩Uyx_n\in U_x\cap U_yxn​∈Ux​∩Uy​ for all n≥Nn\geq Nn≥N. This contradicts the assumption that U∩V=∅U\cap V=\emptysetU∩V=∅.

Therefore, x=yx=yx=y.
