Definition of bounded metric space

A metric space (Y,d)(Y,d)(Y,d) is bounded if there is M∈R≥0M\in\mathbb{R}^{\geq 0}M∈R≥0 such that

∀y,y′∈Y,d(y,y′)<M\forall y,y'\in Y, d(y,y')<M∀y,y′∈Y,d(y,y′)<M

Example of bounded metric space

If (Y,d)(Y,d)(Y,d) is a bounded metric space, let MMM be a positive constant, then d‾=min⁡{M,d}\overline{d}=\min\{M,d\}d=min{M,d} is a bounded metric space.

In fact, the metric topology by ddd and d‾\overline{d}d are the same. (proved in homeworks)
