Chapter 7: Composability

So far we’ve sought security against

c←Enck(m)c\gets Enc_k(m)c←Enck​(m)

Adversary knows ccc, but nothing else.

Attack models

Known plaintext attack (KPA)

Adversary has seen (m1,Enck(m1)),(m2,Enck(m2)),⋯ ,(mq,Enck(mq))(m_1,Enc_k(m_1)),(m_2,Enc_k(m_2)),\cdots,(m_q,Enc_k(m_q))(m1​,Enck​(m1​)),(m2​,Enck​(m2​)),⋯,(mq​,Enck​(mq​)).

m1,⋯ ,mqm_1,\cdots,m_qm1​,⋯,mq​ are known to the adversary.

Given new c=Enck(m)c=Enc_k(m)c=Enck​(m), is previous knowledge helpful?

Chosen plaintext attack (CPA)

Adversary can choose m1,⋯ ,mqm_1,\cdots,m_qm1​,⋯,mq​ and obtain Enck(m1),⋯ ,Enck(mq)Enc_k(m_1),\cdots,Enc_k(m_q)Enck​(m1​),⋯,Enck​(mq​).

Then adversary see new encryption c=Enck(m)c=Enc_k(m)c=Enck​(m). with the same key.

Example:

In WWII, Japan planned to attack “AF”, but US suspected it means Midway.

So US use Axis: Enck(AF)Enc_k(AF)Enck​(AF) and ran out of supplies.

Then US know Japan will attack Midway.

Chosen ciphertext attack (CCA)

Adversary can choose c1,⋯ ,cqc_1,\cdots,c_qc1​,⋯,cq​ and obtain Deck(c1),⋯ ,Deck(cq)Dec_k(c_1),\cdots,Dec_k(c_q)Deck​(c1​),⋯,Deck​(cq​).

Definition 168.1 (Secure private key encryption against attacks)

Capture these ideas with the adversary having oracle access.

Let Π=(Gen,Enc,Dec)\Pi=(Gen,Enc,Dec)Π=(Gen,Enc,Dec) be a private key encryption scheme. Let a random variable INDbO1,O2(Π,A,n)IND_b^{O_1,O_2}(\Pi,\mathcal{A},n)INDbO1​,O2​​(Π,A,n) where A\mathcal{A}A is an n.u.p.p.t. The experiment is the following:

  • Key k←Gen(1n)k\gets Gen(1^n)k←Gen(1n)
  • Adversary AO1(k)(1n)\mathcal{A}^{O_1(k)}(1^n)AO1​(k)(1n) queries oracle O1O_1O1​
  • m0,m1←AO1(k)(1n)m_0,m_1\gets \mathcal{A}^{O_1(k)}(1^n)m0​,m1​←AO1​(k)(1n)
  • c←Enck(mb)c\gets Enc_k(m_b)c←Enck​(mb​)

  1. Show that the modifier version Π′RF\Pi'^{RF}Π′RF where fk2f_{k_2}fk2​​ is replaced with a random function is CCA2 secure.
  2. If ours isn’t, then PRF detector can be created.
