Covering space

Definition of partition into slice

Let p:E→Bp:E\to Bp:E→B be a continuous surjective map. The open set U⊆BU\subseteq BU⊆B is said to be evenly covered by ppp if it’s inverse image p−1(U)p^{-1}(U)p−1(U) can be written as the union of disjoint open sets VαV_\alphaVα​ in EEE. Such that for each α\alphaα, the restriction of ppp to VαV_\alphaVα​ is a homeomorphism of VαV_\alphaVα​ onto UUU.

The collection of {Vα}\{V_\alpha\}{Vα​} is called a partition p−1(U)p^{-1}(U)p−1(U) into slice.

Stack of pancakes ({Vα}\{V_\alpha\}{Vα​}) on plate UUU, each VαV_\alphaVα​ is a pancake homeomorphic to UUU

Note that all the sets in the definition are open.

Definition of covering space

Let p:E→Bp:E\to Bp:E→B be a continuous surjective map. If every point bbb of BBB has a neighborhood evenly covered by ppp, which means p−1(U)p^{-1}(U)p−1(U) is partitioned into slice, then ppp is called a covering map and EEE is called a covering space.

Examples of covering space

identity map is a covering map

Consider the B×Γ→BB\times \Gamma\to BB×Γ→B with Γ\GammaΓ being the discrete topology with the projection map onto BBB.

This is a covering map.

Solving the inverse image for the eiθe^{i\theta}eiθ with ϵ\epsilonϵ interval, we can get nnn slices for each neighborhood of eiθe^{i\theta}eiθ, −ϵ<θ<ϵ-\epsilon< \theta< \epsilon−ϵ<θ<ϵ.

You can continue the computation and find the exact ϵ\epsilonϵ so that the inverse image of p−1p^{-1}p−1 is small and each interval don’t intersect (so that we can make homeomorphism for each interval).

Usually, we don’t choose the UUU to be the whole space.

Consider the projection for the boundary of mobius strip into middle circle.

This is a covering map since the boundary of mobius strip is winding the middle circle twice, and for each point on the middle circle with small enough neighborhood, there will be two disjoint interval on the boundary of mobius strip that are homeomorphic to the middle circle.
