Imbedding of Manifolds

Definition of Manifold

An mmm-dimensional manifold is a topological space XXX that is

  1. Hausdorff
  2. With a countable basis
  3. Each point of xxx of XXX has a neighborhood that is homeomorphic to an open subset of Rm\mathbb{R}^mRm. (local euclidean)
Note

Try to find some example that satisfies some of the properties above but not a manifold.

  1. Non-Hausdorff
    • Real line with two origin, as discussed in homework problem
  2. Non-countable basis
    • Consider Rδ\mathbb{R}^\deltaRδ where the set is R\mathbb{R}R with discrete topology. The basis must include all singleton sets in R\mathbb{R}R therefore Rδ\mathbb{R}^\deltaRδ is not second countable.
  3. Non-local euclidean
    • Consider the subspace topology over segment [0,1][0,1][0,1] on real line, the subspace topology is not local euclidean since the open set containing the end point [0,a)[0,a)[0,a) is not homeomorphic to open sets in R\mathbb{R}R. (if we remove the end point, in the segment space we have (0,a)(0,a)(0,a) but in R\mathbb{R}R is (−a,0)∪(0,a)(-a,0)\cup (0,a)(−a,0)∪(0,a), which is not connected. Therefore cannot be homeomorphic to open sets in R\mathbb{R}R)
    • Any shape with intersection is not local euclidean.

Whitney’s Embedding Theorem

If XXX is a compact mmm-manifold, then XXX can be imbedded in RN\mathbb{R}^NRN for some positive integer NNN.

In general, XXX is not required to be compact. And NNN is not too big. For non compact XXX, N≤2m+1N\leq 2m+1N≤2m+1 and for compact XXX, N≤2mN\leq 2mN≤2m.

Existence of finite partition of unity
