Math4202 Topology II (Lecture 7)

Algebraic Topology

Classify 2-dimensional topological manifolds (connected) up to homeomorphism/homotopy equivalence.

Use fundamental groups.

We want to show that:

  1. The fundamental group is invariant under the equivalence relation.
  2. develop some methods to compute the groups.
  3. 2-dimensional topological spaces with the same fundamental group are equivalent (homeomorphism).

Homotopy of paths

Definition of path

If fff and f′f'f′ are two continuous maps from XXX to YYY, where XXX and YYY are topological spaces. Then we say that fff is homotopic to f′f'f′ if there exists a continuous map F:X×[0,1]→YF:X\times [0,1]\to YF:X×[0,1]→Y such that F(x,0)=f(x)F(x,0)=f(x)F(x,0)=f(x) and F(x,1)=f′(x)F(x,1)=f'(x)F(x,1)=f′(x) for all x∈Xx\in Xx∈X.

The map FFF is called a homotopy between fff and f′f'f′.

We use f≃f′f\simeq f'f≃f′ to mean that fff is homotopic to f′f'f′.

Definition of homotopic equivalence map

Let f:X→Yf:X\to Yf:X→Y and g:Y→Xg:Y\to Xg:Y→X be two continuous maps. If f∘g:Y→Yf\circ g:Y\to Yf∘g:Y→Y and g∘f:X→Xg\circ f:X\to Xg∘f:X→X are homotopic to the identity maps id⁡Y\operatorname{id}_YidY​ and id⁡X\operatorname{id}_XidX​, then fff and ggg are homotopic equivalence maps. And the two spaces XXX and YYY are homotopy equivalent.

Note

This condition is weaker than homeomorphism. (In homeomorphism, let g=f−1g=f^{-1}g=f−1, we require g∘f=id⁡Xg\circ f=\operatorname{id}_Xg∘f=idX​ and f∘g=id⁡Yf\circ g=\operatorname{id}_Yf∘g=idY​.)

Example of homotopy equivalence maps

Let X={a}X=\{a\}X={a} and Y=[0,1]Y=[0,1]Y=[0,1] with standard topology.

Consider f:X→Yf:X\to Yf:X→Y by f(a)=0f(a)=0f(a)=0 and g:Y→Xg:Y\to Xg:Y→X by g(y)=ag(y)=ag(y)=a, where y∈[0,1]y\in [0,1]y∈[0,1].

g∘f=id⁡Xg\circ f=\operatorname{id}_Xg∘f=idX​ and f∘g=[0,1]↦0f\circ g=[0,1]\mapsto 0f∘g=[0,1]↦0.

g∘f≃id⁡Xg\circ f\simeq \operatorname{id}_Xg∘f≃idX​

and f∘g≃id⁡Yf\circ g\simeq \operatorname{id}_Yf∘g≃idY​.

Consider F:X×[0,1]→YF:X\times [0,1]\to YF:X×[0,1]→Y by F(a,0)=0F(a,0)=0F(a,0)=0 and F(a,t)=(1−t)yF(a,t)=(1-t)yF(a,t)=(1−t)y. FFF is continuous and homotopy between f∘gf\circ gf∘g and id⁡Y\operatorname{id}_YidY​.

This gives example of homotopy but not homeomorphism.

Definition of null homology

If f:X→Yf:X\to Yf:X→Y is homotopy to a constant map. fff is called null homotopy.

Definition of path homotopy

Let f,f′:I→Xf,f':I\to Xf,f′:I→X be a continuous maps from an interval I=[0,1]I=[0,1]I=[0,1] to a topological space XXX.

Two pathes fff and f′f'f′ are path homotopic if
