Theorem for the fundamental group of wedged nnn circle

Let XXX be the wedge of nnn circles.

We claim that π1(X,x0)=Fn\pi_1(X,x_0)=F_nπ1​(X,x0​)=Fn​. (Free group generated by nnn generators.)

Proof

We proceed by induction on nnn,

When n=1n=1n=1, then π1(X,x0)=⟨a⟩\pi_1(X,x_0)=\langle a\rangleπ1​(X,x0​)=⟨a⟩.

Let X=S1∪S2∪⋯∪SnX=S_1\cup S_2\cup\cdots\cup S_nX=S1​∪S2​∪⋯∪Sn​, where SiS_iSi​ is the iii-th circle.

Let W_i=S_1\\{p_i\} where pi≠pp_i\neq ppi​=p, where we wedged the circle at ppp.

Let

U=W1∪W2∪⋯∪Wn−1∪SnU=W_1\cup W_2\cup\cdots\cup W_{n-1}\cup S_nU=W1​∪W2​∪⋯∪Wn−1​∪Sn​ V=S1∪S2∪⋯∪Sn−1∪WnV=S_1\cup S_2\cup\cdots\cup S_{n-1}\cup W_nV=S1​∪S2​∪⋯∪Sn−1​∪Wn​

And U∩VU\cap VU∩V is path connected, and simply connected, therefore π1(U∩V,x0)=Z\pi_1(U\cap V,x_0)=\mathbb Zπ1​(U∩V,x0​)=Z.
